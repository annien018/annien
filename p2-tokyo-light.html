Prototyping a Portable Lamp for SFU Students

Academic Project // Canada // 2018 // Team of 4

My responsibility: Technical Illustration, Persona Analysis

Adobe IllustratorAdobe Photoshop

Objective

We, a team of 4, designed and fabricated a table lamp for Simon Fraser University that has to be feasible, functional, and aesthetic.

Figure 1: Final Product

Concern

Many study areas in SFU Surrey often lack light at night. The lamp must improve the working environment of students.

Feasibility Consideration

The small and minimal design gave a budget-friendly product and is easily portable. We also considered the materials to create a long-lasting product.

Challenge

The product should provoke an emotional response from the users to grab their interest.

Solution

We decided to go with the greenery motif, the metaphorical bamboo, and the leaf shapes since it would be easily identifiable to our users as they can feel familiarity with the product from the first look.

Design Problem

The lamp should have transparency to encourage user interaction and limit the confusion.

Refinement of Design

The rod is made longer compared to the original drawing to suggest it can be held to move around. With three similar placeholders near it indicates a connection. The two-button shapes near the light suggest the consequence of pushing them will relate to the light itself. Its twin-liked look also indicates that it can be used both at the same time.

Final Reflection

From this project, I learned about the importance of design semantics. It definitely takes time to gain a deeper understanding of the context of a design, but, in the end, it adds more value and a higher standard to the outcome of a product.
