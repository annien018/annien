Prototyping a Portable Lamp for SFU Students

Academic Project // Canada // 2018 // Team of 4

My responsibility: Technical Illustration, Persona Analysis, Poster's Visual Layout

Adobe IllustratorAdobe Photoshop

Objective

We, a team of 4, designed and fabricated a table lamp for Simon Fraser University that has to be feasible, functional, and aesthetic. I managed the technical illustration to show the lamp in detailed 2D parts. I also created the persona board to explain to the professor and TA our inspiration in designing the lamp's function, shape, and colour.

Figure 1: Final Product

Design Problem

The lamp should have transparency to encourage user interaction and limit the confusion.

Figure 2: Persona sheet.

Refinement of Design

The lamp's rod is longer compared to the original illustration to suggest it can be held to move around.

Figure 3: Technical illustration.

Figure 4: Final poster for print.

Final Reflection

From this project, I learned about the importance of design semantics. It definitely takes time to gain a deeper understanding of the context of a design, but, in the end, it adds more value and a higher standard to the outcome of a product.

I also learned that for printing, I should use CMYK setting in Illustrator with at least 300dpi for high printing resolution.
